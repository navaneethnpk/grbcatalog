AstroSat CZTI

Astrosat's CZTI (Cadmium Zinc Telluride Imager) instrument is a hard X-ray detector that can detect high-energy photons in the energy range of 10 keV to 100 keV. CZTI consists of a coded mask and an array of pixel detectors, which enables it to capture high-resolution images of the sky in this energy range.

One of the key scientific objectives of CZTI is to study the transient and variable phenomena in the universe, such as gamma-ray bursts (GRBs). CZTI has detected several GRBs since its launch in 2015, including a record-breaking detection of the shortest GRB with a duration of only 0.4 seconds.

The GRB detections by CZTI have contributed significantly to our understanding of these powerful cosmic explosions.
